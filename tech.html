8 Peronal Favorite Tech Items (from Lazada)

These are some personal tech pieces that are used everyday and usually because they are good enough to great products. So here is a list from good to great, in my opinion. Sorry in advance for the quality of the photos.

8) The Razer Mouse Pad


This mouse pad was nice, a lot of space for your mouse to move and extra space to place your keyboard. The only issue I can think of personally is that it is most likely not a genuine product since Razer products are known for a hefty price tag, but this is the only mouse pad that I've purchased and have been using every since

7) Blue Light Filter Glasses


These glasses are a bit annoying to wear because they are crooked. A lot of reviews have stated this as well. It may be due to the poor packaging, but this is higher on the list because I do care about my eyesight. Since online classes are happening, students will have more time exposed to blue light than before, so this should hopefully help with that

6) Haylou Solar Smartwatch


This watch does not look like the price range that it is in. It has a lot of features for the price. The gripe I have with this watch though is that the software is buggy. Haylou is a company that works with Xiaomi, and I have tried their mi bands before. They have better software, but I don't like the frame of the Mi Band line personally. So if somehow they catch up with xiaomi, they would easily be higher on the list

5) BESDER Webcam


A classmate of mine started streaming and I thought why not try it as well, during the summer. Searching through webcams I came accross this and after a few streams of mine, my friend asked what the webcam model was. To his suprise it wasn't a logitech c920. This also as a viewing angle of 130 degrees (according to the page). This webcam is good if you ever need to have a camera in front of you such as online class.

4) Speaker


Again, for the price they sound good. I've purchased this not too long ago and I am pleased with the amount of volume this can output. This won't compare with the high end JBL's and Bose speakers, but you could buy multiple of these speakers for one of those. If you're looking for just a speaker on the desk then these shouldn't have a problem

3) Redragon Headset


This headset was recommended by my cousin and sure enough it doesn't dissappoint. It sacrifices on the quality of the mic, but you have a 7.1 Surround sound a volume slider, and a vibration feature. The vibration feature sometimes helps when playing games, because when the sounds comming from the game emit a lot of bass the headset kinda rumbles, thus adding into the emmersion.

2) MiBox S


I don't have a tv in my room, and I don't want to open the pc just to watch some online video content, because of the power consumption. So this tiny thing has a pack of features such as Youtube, Netflix, Amazon Prime, Chrome cast and more. Linus tech tips reviewed this product, and comapred to the competitors, it is competitively priced.

1) Logitech G304


Around Youtube, recommendations of wireless mice emerged on my feed. Browsing around and they all have one conlusion "go wireless". The reviews claim that wireless mice nowadays are indistinguisable in terms of input lag, compared to wired mice. Seems to good to be true, but looking at experiments done, it seems like the claims may be right. When switching from wired to wireless, it can get pretty pricey. This mouse is one of the cheapest wireless mice you'll find. In my experience, the first time holding the mouse was weird. I didn't feel any restrictions due to there being no wire present, so everytime I'd play games, it felt like the mouse would fall off my desk. And if ever I was lazy I could sit back from a far and just bring my mouse and use my computer from a distance. And sure enough it is indistinguisable to other wired mice.
